# Video 5 — Managing mallams, learners, and delivery follow-up

**Audience:** implementation leads, center ops, LMS admins, mallam supervisors  
**Target runtime:** 10-12 minutes  
**Goal:** teach day-to-day operator flows for mallam oversight, learner assignment, roster changes, and follow-up after content goes live.

## Outcome

By the end of this video, the viewer should be able to:

- understand what to read on learner and mallam pages
- assign and reassign learners to mallams
- monitor roster pressure and attendance signals
- use progress and rewards as follow-up surfaces after delivery

## Run of show

### Segment 1 — Why mallam and learner ops matter
**Narration:**
> Good curriculum means nothing if the delivery layer is chaos. Mallam and learner pages are where admins see who is carrying the work, who needs support, and where roster or attendance problems are building.

### Segment 2 — Learners page
**Narration:**
> Start on the learners page when you need to understand an individual learner’s status, cohort, progression, assignment context, or support needs.

### Segment 3 — Mallams page
**Narration:**
> Use the mallams page to read operator load, attendance spread, watchlist pressure, and delivery history. The goal is not just to see names. It is to understand capacity and risk.

### Segment 4 — Assigning a learner to a mallam
**Narration:**
> On the mallam profile, use the roster manager to add a learner directly. If the learner already belongs to another mallam, this flow reassigns them. That makes roster correction much faster than bouncing through multiple pages.

### Segment 5 — Reassigning or removing a learner
**Narration:**
> In the current roster section, you can update ownership by moving the learner to another mallam or setting them to unassigned. This is useful for balancing load, handling transitions, or cleaning up bad roster state.

### Segment 6 — Reading pressure and attendance
**Narration:**
> Do not just look at totals. Read the posture: roster size, attendance rate, stable versus at-risk learners, and delivery timeline. One mallam with a large roster and dropping attendance is not the same story as one with a small but stable group.

### Segment 7 — Progress and rewards follow-up
**Narration:**
> After lessons go live, use progress and rewards to verify what happened downstream. Progress shows readiness and intervention logic. Rewards shows XP, badges, and manual corrections when recovery is needed.

### Segment 8 — Reports and escalation
**Narration:**
> Reports gives the wider program view: pod risk, mallam contribution, compliance checks, and external reporting. Use it to spot escalation patterns that are larger than one learner or one center.

## Demo script for clicks

1. Open **Learners** and explain what the list is for.
2. Open one **learner profile** and describe what admins should check first.
3. Open **Mallams** and then a **mallam detail** page.
4. Scroll to **Add learner to this mallam** and explain the reassignment behavior.
5. Scroll to **Manage current roster** and show the ownership update selector.
6. Explain when to use **unassigned**.
7. Point out roster pressure, attendance, and watchlist context.
8. Open **Progress** and explain progression follow-up.
9. Open **Rewards** and explain what manual correction is for.
10. Open **Reports** and explain pod or program-level escalation.

## Presenter notes

- Keep this firmly operational.
- Emphasize that mallam pages are not static bios; they are decision surfaces.
- Be explicit that rewards is not a shortcut for promoting an unready learner.

---

## Companion narration block for reassignment flow

> The roster manager is built for real operations, not just neat profiles. You can add a learner directly to this mallam, move a learner to another mallam, or remove them from the roster entirely. Use those controls to keep delivery load realistic instead of letting bad roster state linger for weeks.
