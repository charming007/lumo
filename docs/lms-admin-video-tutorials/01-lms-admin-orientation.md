# Video 1 — LMS admin orientation and system flow

**Audience:** new LMS admins, implementation leads, operators  
**Target runtime:** 9-11 minutes  
**Goal:** give admins the correct mental model for how the LMS, content, assignments, learner runtime, rewards, and intervention surfaces connect.

## Outcome

By the end of this video, the viewer should understand:

- what the LMS is actually for
- where the main routes live
- how content moves from authoring to delivery
- how learner activity returns as progress, reward, and intervention signals

## Run of show

### Segment 1 — What this LMS is for
**On screen:** LMS guide page or dashboard  
**Narration:**
> Lumo’s LMS is not just a reporting dashboard. It is the admin control surface for curriculum structure, lesson authoring, release readiness, mallam operations, learner progress, and reporting. If you’re treating it like a passive analytics wall, you’re already using it wrong.

### Segment 2 — Main navigation and route map
**On screen:** sidebar navigation, Guide page route cards  
**Narration:**
> The core routes to understand first are Dashboard, Content Library, English Studio, learners, mallams, progress, rewards, reports, and settings. Each route has a different job. Content Library defines what exists. English Studio improves English lessons. Lesson Studio builds the full lesson payload. Mallam and learner pages show who is carrying the work. Reports package the evidence for external review.

### Segment 3 — Dashboard as the operational cockpit
**On screen:** dashboard cards, operations pulse, escalations  
**Narration:**
> The Dashboard is where admins answer the urgent questions fast: who is active, what is blocked, who is at risk, and who is ready to progress. This is the right page for triage, not detailed authoring.

### Segment 4 — Content Library as the curriculum spine
**On screen:** Content Library board  
**Narration:**
> Content Library is the canonical curriculum spine. Subjects, strands, modules, lessons, and assessment gates live here. If the structure is broken here, the rest of the system inherits the mess.

### Segment 5 — English Studio and Lesson Studio
**On screen:** English Studio, then Lesson Studio  
**Narration:**
> English Studio is the English-specific authoring layer. It helps authors build better English lessons with objectives, vocabulary, activity sequence, and readiness checks. Lesson Studio is the full payload editor. That’s where interactive steps, answer choices, media cues, assessment items, and facilitation notes get defined properly.

### Segment 6 — Delivery and learner feedback loop
**On screen:** assignments page, learner or progress surfaces  
**Narration:**
> Once approved content is assigned, the learner runtime produces the signals that matter: completion, mastery, progression state, and intervention flags. Those signals come back into the LMS through dashboard, progress, rewards, and learner views.

### Segment 7 — Mallams, rewards, and reports
**On screen:** mallam profile, rewards board, reports page  
**Narration:**
> Mallam pages show operator load, roster pressure, and delivery history. Rewards reinforces verified milestones through XP, badges, and levels. Reports packages the same evidence into NGO, donor, and ministry-ready readouts.

### Segment 8 — Sane admin workflow
**On screen:** Guide page workflow section  
**Narration:**
> The clean workflow is simple: structure content first, attach the assessment gate, author the lesson properly, publish only when the readiness checks are real, assign it, then monitor learner and mallam performance after release.

## Presenter notes

- Do not dive too deep into lesson creation yet.
- Keep this mental-model-first.
- Use plain language: structure, authoring, delivery, feedback, intervention.
- Call out that rewards support progress but do not replace mastery.
